# Equity-Research-Prompt-Chain-for-505(b)(2)-Pathway-Drug-Applications
Practice Methodology for LLM-Driven Pharmaceutical Equity Research

Prompt 1: 
Identify the primary high-impact binary risk factor for **** over the next 12 months (e.g., PDUFA, Phase 3 readout, patent cliff, or debt maturity). Search the most recent 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K filings to locate the specific technical hurdle that has previously caused a delay or setback (e.g., a CMC deficiency, a clinical hold, or a supply chain 'material weakness'). Summarize the company’s official narrative versus the specific technical reason cited by the regulator or auditor.

Prompt 2: 
Regarding the **** identified, generate a list of investigative 'red-flag' questions.
Where is the specific physical location or third-party entity (CDMO, Law Firm, Auditor) responsible for this hurdle?
What is the regulatory track record of that specific location/entity (search for Form 483s, EIRs, or previous litigation)?
Are there discrepancies between different regulatory bodies regarding this entity (e.g., US-FDA vs. EU-EMA)?
Identify 3 historical precedents of other companies that failed or succeeded at this exact same hurdle.

Prompt 3:
Subject: Structural Integrity Audit of Regarding
Objective: Perform a sober, investigative analysis to determine if the company’s recovery strategy for **** is a 'paper fix' or a meaningful operational pivot.
Task 1: Sufficiency of the Evidence Base. Analyze the underlying data/evidence package. Is the 'bridge' to success (e.g., a 505(b)(2) pathway or a legal precedent) robust enough to withstand a secondary challenge? Evaluate if the medical/legal review is functionally 'closed,' leaving only the operational hurdle.
Task 2: Systemic vs. Niche Risk. Investigate the anatomy of the previous failure. Is the issue a 'niche' procedural gap specific to one regulator’s documentation standards, or does it suggest a systemic inability in the supply chain or legal strategy? Research recent site inspections or court rulings for the specific entities involved.
Task 3: Operational Resilience & 'Plan B'. Evaluate the company’s remediation strategy. Is the qualification of a backup vendor/legal team a standard risk-mitigation step or an emergency 'Hail Mary'? Determine if this 'Plan B' truly de-risks the upcoming ****.
Final Deliverable: Provide a 'Go/No-Go' assessment on the likelihood of the catalyst based on whether the hurdle is localized and remediable or structural and threatening. Conclude with a 'Stress Test' of the cash runway—can they survive a second potential delay?
